# LLM Wiki Ideas

A synthesis of questions, critiques, conceptual extensions, and implementation lessons from the Karpathy LLM Wiki gist comment thread — organized around the core pattern described in `llm-wiki.md`. Each section identifies a gap or opportunity, summarizes what the community raised, and points to concrete solutions that emerged.

---

## 1. Epistemic Integrity

The thread's deepest concern: an LLM-maintained wiki can synthesize without citing, drift from its sources without knowing it, and present false certainty where sources actually disagree.

### The Problem

- **Persistent errors compound.** Unlike stateless LLM hallucinations that reset per prompt, a wiki mistake becomes a prior that future compilations build on. ([308.md](comments/308.md))
- **False coherence.** The wiki can present a confident synthesis where the underlying sources actually disagree. Lint catches surface contradictions, but not paraphrasing-bias or subtly merged claims. ([328.md](comments/328.md), [161.md](comments/161.md))
- **Cache poisoning.** The wiki is a cache of compiled knowledge — and like any cache, it can be poisoned. Validation at every step is mandatory. ([166.md](comments/166.md))
- **ETH Zurich research** found LLM-generated context files hurt agent performance in 5 of 8 settings. ([344.md](comments/344.md))

### Proposed Solutions

- **Claim typing.** After 6 months in production, one team shipped four claim types as Obsidian callouts: **Source** (verbatim quote with citation), **Analysis** (inference from sourced facts with reasoning shown), **Unverified** (no authoritative source yet), **Gap** (explicitly missing — never fill with a plausible guess). The Analysis/Unverified split prevents paraphrasing-bias. ([374.md](comments/374.md))
- **Five epistemic constraints.** Separate facts/inferences/open questions; require source links; make ingest idempotent; LLM proposes diffs not overwrites; lint for stale and unsupported claims. ([037.md](comments/037.md))
- **Training period.** Humans review all wiki writes for the first ~30 days, after which automated linting takes over. The system learns your conventions, then stops asking. ([344.md](comments/344.md))
- **Multi-model verification.** Route questions to 4 frontier LLMs, synthesize into Knowledge Units with agreement/dispute tracking. Cryptographic receipt chains so every unit is verifiable offline. (Veritas Acta, [038.md](comments/038.md))
- **LLM-as-a-judge.** Use one LLM to evaluate another's wiki edits. ([063.md](comments/063.md))

### Key Implementations

- **Veritas Acta** ([038.md](comments/038.md)): Multi-model verification with cryptographic receipts, formalized as an IETF Internet-Draft.
- **Freelance** ([085.md](comments/085.md)): Content hash tracking per source file; stale propositions detected at query time.
- **FUNGI protocol** ([379.md](comments/379.md)): A note-processing framework — Frame, Unearth, Network, Grow, Interrogate — with an explicit rule: "No smoothing. Preserve contradictions rather than resolving them for neatness."

## 2. Provenance & Staleness Detection

The thread's most compelling architectural innovation: tracking not just what the wiki knows but which source produced each claim and whether that source has changed.

### The Problem

- Without provenance, the wiki can silently go stale. A source changes, and 15 derived pages are now wrong but don't know it.
- **Cascading invalidation** is unresolved: if a source changes, do you re-derive all pages that depend on it? Hash-based provenance detects staleness but doesn't prescribe the recompilation strategy. ([087.md](comments/087.md))

### Proposed Solutions

- **Hash-based provenance.** Every proposition records source files and their content hashes at compilation time. Queries validate freshness via hash check. Stale = mismatch. ([085.md](comments/085.md))
- **Git-native provenance.** SHA-256 hashing on every source at ingest. Every idea gets a commit — creating timestamped proofs of discovery, priority, and derivation. (paper-spec, [123.md](comments/123.md))
- **Git blame on every fact.** Deterministic executor validates LLM-proposed operations (KEEP, UPDATE, MERGE, SUPERSEDE, ARCHIVE) as JSON before committing. (Palinode, [084.md](comments/084.md))
- **Parallel provenance layer.** "The wiki is the codebase, Anamnesis is the git log." Tracks how knowledge was compiled, why decisions were made, what superseded what. (Anamnesis, [089.md](comments/089.md))
- **Issuer-blind cryptographic receipts** to prove wiki content was actually generated by the claimed model. Critical for shared, high-stakes knowledge bases. ([322.md](comments/322.md))

### The `reflect` Step

One of the most concrete proposals: add a `reflect` step to the loop — `ingest -> compile -> reflect -> query -> lint` — where the wiki records not just what changed but *why*, creating decision records as first-class pages. Makes the wiki able to trace why one concept became a hub while another stayed peripheral. ([088.md](comments/088.md))

## 3. Scaling Beyond the Index File

The gist says the index-file approach "works surprisingly well at moderate scale (~100 sources, ~hundreds of pages)." The thread explored what happens past that.

### The Problem

- The index file stops being navigable past ~200 pages. Retrieval, not storage, is where the filesystem approach fails.
- Whether *compilation quality* degrades at scale (not just retrieval) is untested.
- Enterprise PDFs (tables, images, 100-300 manuals) may be infeasible. ([035.md](comments/035.md))
- Memory drift: as knowledge grows, how to keep it coherent? ([318.md](comments/318.md))

### Proposed Solutions

**Search infrastructure (keep markdown, add a search layer):**
- **Hybrid BM25 + vector search** via SQLite-vec. 227 files, 2,230 indexed chunks in production. (Palinode, [084.md](comments/084.md))
- **Local semantic search** over markdown. 95.4% on LongMemEval at 50ms retrieval, packaged as an Obsidian plugin. (OMEGA, [178.md](comments/178.md))
- **Structure-aware tree search** using heading hierarchy. Zero ML models, 5,000 docs in under 10ms. (TreeSearch, [244.md](comments/244.md))
- **DNS-inspired typed records.** 83x faster than grep on 14k files. Agent reads ~242x fewer files. (blink-query, [235.md](comments/235.md))

**Database escape hatches (move beyond flat files for retrieval):**
- **SQLite as index.** After 6-12 months of filesystem experience: "the best abstraction for agents at scale." ([092.md](comments/092.md))
- **SQLite transaction log** that *renders* as markdown. Edits in any editor flow back into the data layer. (Binder, [054.md](comments/054.md))
- **Typed JSON contracts** (not free-form markdown) make output stable across LLM providers. Lint becomes a SQL query. (WikiMind, [264.md](comments/264.md))

**Deterministic alternatives (no LLM needed for retrieval):**
- **Compiler API extraction.** No LLM at all — deterministic extraction using TypeScript compiler APIs and regex. 200ms, zero API cost, cannot hallucinate. (codesight, [219.md](comments/219.md))

**Federated indexing:**
- Instead of one massive index, use per-project indexes. Each major track has its own `index.md`. ([141.md](comments/141.md))
- Keep separate wikis per major domain, with cross-reference via links but not a unified index. ([360.md](comments/360.md))

### The Pragmatic Synthesis

Markdown as source of truth, SQLite (or similar) as index. Start with flat files, add infrastructure only when retrieval becomes a bottleneck (~200+ pages).

## 4. Wiki Structure & Organization

Practical questions the gist intentionally leaves open — and the answers that emerged.

### Page Granularity

- **When to create a new page vs. edit an existing one?** ([003.md](comments/003.md)) — The gist is deliberately silent. Several implementations introduced entity registries to prevent duplicate pages. ([382.md](comments/382.md))
- **Entity type templates.** One template per entity type, not one generic template. Seven types as sweet spot. ([048.md](comments/048.md))
- **Type schemas.** Some argued the wiki needs a formal type schema to classify information. ([280.md](comments/280.md))

### Token Management

- **Index token budget.** L0 ~200 tokens through L3 5-20K. ([048.md](comments/048.md))
- **TLDRs at the top of every page.** LLMs scan the TLDR first and decide whether to read the full article. Saves significant tokens. ([028.md](comments/028.md))
- **Surgically small index.** Claude Code's compaction problem: whatever exceeds the context budget gets deprioritized silently. Fix: keep the index surgically small. ([372.md](comments/372.md))

### Classification & Preprocessing

- **Classify documents by type before extracting.** Saves tokens, improves quality. ([048.md](comments/048.md))
- **Pre-generate image descriptions** so LLMs process text + visual context in one pass. ([012.md](comments/012.md))

### Maintenance

- **Staleness scoring** for periodic pruning. ([374.md](comments/374.md))
- **Spaced-repetition review** for knowledge reinforcement. ([162.md](comments/162.md))
- **Every task produces two outputs** — the deliverable and wiki updates. ([048.md](comments/048.md))
- **Design for cross-domain from day one.** Shared entities become hub nodes. ([048.md](comments/048.md))

### Logging

- **Use git instead of log.md.** Log files confuse less intelligent models. ([200.md](comments/200.md))
- **Skip the index file entirely;** just instruct the LLM to `ls` the directory. ([278.md](comments/278.md))

## 5. The Human Role

A set of critiques about what's lost when the LLM does the writing, and proposals for keeping the human meaningfully in the loop.

### The Generation Effect Critique

- **Outsourcing the writing costs understanding.** Active processing (not passive LLM summaries) is what drives recognition and comprehension. ([165.md](comments/165.md))
- **"If the LLM authors, you've built a personalized research index, not a second brain."** The act of writing is load-bearing in PKM — removing it changes the nature of the system. ([337.md](comments/337.md))
- A compromise: entries require personal thinking first; the LLM challenges understanding before filing. ([271.md](comments/271.md))

### The Editor-in-Chief Model

- **"The LLM is the writer. You're the editor-in-chief."** ([048.md](comments/048.md))
- The schema file is the real flywheel: every correction you give the LLM gets filed back so it never repeats the same mistake. ([156.md](comments/156.md))
- "You don't need to be a developer to build this." — Validated in enterprise service delivery management. ([156.md](comments/156.md))

### Autonomy vs. Involvement

- The gist emphasizes human involvement ("I prefer to ingest sources one at a time and stay involved"). Many implementations pushed toward full autonomy — autopilot modes, cron-based maintenance, autonomous research loops.
- The tension: autonomous systems compound errors as well as knowledge.
- Predicted evolution: from wiki to 24/7 autonomous self-evolving agents. ([079.md](comments/079.md))

## 6. Multi-Agent & Team Use

The pattern is designed for a single agent and personal use. The thread explored what breaks when you add more agents or more humans.

### Problems

- File-level locking is insufficient when agents operate on overlapping concepts.
- Multiple LLMs editing the same wiki may not maintain consistency.
- Access control, review gates, and the question of "who can edit what" are unresolved for teams.

### Proposed Solutions

- **Git repo sharing.** The simplest answer: the wiki is just a git repo of markdown files. Share the repo. ([004.md](comments/004.md))
- **TTL-based mutual exclusion** with wiki locking and contamination firewalls. (multi-agent-wiki-toolkit, [359.md](comments/359.md))
- **Consensus voting** (75% threshold, Devil's Advocate required). RSA-PSS signed knowledge commits on a tamper-proof DAG. (DPC Messenger, [194.md](comments/194.md))
- **Trust tiers and relationship-typed links.** Consolidation engine that scores, merges, and prunes rather than just appending. (Automaton Memory System, [332.md](comments/332.md))
- **Postgres gateway** for multi-agent distributed sharing. (agentic-coop-db, [353.md](comments/353.md))
- **Specialized agent architecture.** Research, audit, context update, and safety check agents running in parallel. MD files as shared language, Obsidian as human dashboard, SQLite as speed layer. ([150.md](comments/150.md))
- **Sandboxed agents** with a 12-section session format to eliminate re-orientation overhead. ([153.md](comments/153.md))

## 7. Conceptual Extensions

Ideas that push the pattern beyond what the gist describes.

### Knowledge Graphs

Flat markdown with wikilinks is an implicit graph. Making it explicit enables operations markdown alone can't support:

- **Typed edges** (supports, contradicts, evolved_into, depends_on) with temporal decay — values hold, ideas fade fast. (thinking-mcp, [099.md](comments/099.md))
- **Community detection, god-node analysis, contradiction edges.** (SwarmVault, [212.md](comments/212.md))
- **Formal OWL-RL ontology with SPARQL.** Deterministic reasoning — no LLM calls for inference. Transitive chain tracing. (Cortex, [333.md](comments/333.md))
- **Embedding graph where the query vector mutates at each hop** (induced-fit retrieval). On HotpotQA with 5.2M articles: all traditional RAG scored 0% Hit@20 on multi-hop questions; this found targets. (celestix-ifr, [262.md](comments/262.md))
- **Persistent graph edges** in `/.connections` for cross-references. Graph traversal (pagerank, neighborhood) for discovery. (grover, [259.md](comments/259.md))

### Decision-Based Learning Loops

- **"Write-back alone doesn't complete the learning loop."** Proposed: task -> options -> decision -> outcome -> memory -> better options next time. A shift from "second brain" to decision system. ([211.md](comments/211.md))
- MCP-based system where the LLM proposes whether a correction is worth adding as a permanent "learning." ([196.md](comments/196.md))

### Compile-Time vs. Query-Time

- **Query-time compilation.** Build knowledge on demand and cache it. Solves the staleness problem (never serve pre-compiled knowledge without checking freshness) but trades latency. (Freelance, [085.md](comments/085.md))
- **Wiki + RAG combined never lost.** Head-to-head tests across 7 evaluation rounds. Also discovered the "Ingestion Gap": a mostly-finished wiki performed 17% worse than a fully compiled one. ([320.md](comments/320.md))

### Identity-Aware Filtering

- The same raw sources, passed through different role filters (founder vs. investor vs. engineer), should produce different wiki pages. The wiki becomes personalized not just by content but by perspective. ([299.md](comments/299.md), [300.md](comments/300.md))

### A Fourth Layer

- Three layers (raw, wiki, schema) wasn't enough in practice. A fourth — an **infrastructure layer** with design records for the agents, rules, hooks, and conventions themselves. The schema needs its own rationale layer when it becomes non-trivial. ([374.md](comments/374.md))

### The Wiki as Agent State Machine

- **Perpetual thinking wikis** — fire up an empty wiki and tell it to autonomously research a topic. ([289.md](comments/289.md))
- **Externalize reasoning into files** (plan, findings, progress) for agent task execution, not just knowledge storage. "Writing is thinking. Re-reading is remembering." ([371.md](comments/371.md))
- **Wiki as semantic cache** — stores distilled answers, reducing repeated LLM calls. ([381.md](comments/381.md))

### Social Knowledge Networks

- A social network of personal knowledge graphs. Public sections enable "standing on shoulders of giants." Git-based forking enables cross-validation of knowledge claims. ([364.md](comments/364.md))

## 9. Contradictions in the Gist

Four internal tensions identified in the gist itself ([370.md](comments/370.md)):

1. **"No RAG" claim vs. later BM25/vector search suggestions.** The gist says the wiki avoids RAG, then recommends qmd (hybrid search with re-ranking).
2. **"LLM writes everything" vs. human edits schema.** The schema layer is co-evolved by human and LLM, blurring the ownership line.
3. **Immutable raw sources vs. image download instructions.** Downloading images modifies the raw layer.
4. **Lint detects contradictions — but who resolves them?** The gist prescribes detection but not resolution.

## 11. Open Questions

Genuinely unresolved issues from the thread:

1. **When does compilation quality degrade?** The thread identified ~200 pages as a retrieval ceiling, but whether the *quality of synthesis* degrades at scale is untested.

2. **Cascading invalidation strategy.** Hash-based provenance detects staleness. What's the right recompilation policy — re-derive all 15 dependent pages, or lazy-recompile on next access?

3. **Can the wiki surface its own uncertainty?** Can an LLM-written wiki reliably distinguish "I synthesized this confidently" from "the underlying sources actually disagree here"?

4. **Right granularity.** New page vs. edit existing, one wiki vs. many, flat vs. hierarchical — domain-dependent, no convergence in the thread.

5. **Team-scale viability.** Access control, review gates, consistency across multiple LLM editors — early implementations exist but are unproven.

6. **The Generation Effect tradeoff.** Does outsourcing the writing to an LLM cost the human meaningful understanding? Is there a hybrid model that preserves both efficiency and comprehension?

7. **Autonomous error compounding.** As systems move toward autonomous operation (cron-based maintenance, autopilot), how do you prevent errors from compounding without human review?
